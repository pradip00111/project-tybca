Apple Nutrition and Benefits

Nutrients: A medium apple contains fiber, vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. It's high in vitamin C, which helps the immune system and improves iron absorption. Apples also contain phytochemicals like quercetin, catechin, chlorogenic acid, and epicatechin, which have antioxidant properties.

Heart health: Apples may help lower cholesterol and blood pressure. The soluble fiber in apples can help prevent cholesterol buildup in blood vessel walls.

Diabetes: Apples may help lower blood sugar levels and reduce the risk of type 2 diabetes. The polyphenol quercetin in apples may explain this effect.

Other health benefits: Apples may also:

  • Support weight loss
  • Promote gut health
  • Help prevent cancer
  • Help fight asthma
  • Protect your brain

You can eat apples as a snack or add them to sweet and savory dishes. All apples are healthy, but some varieties, like Red Delicious, Granny Smith, and Gala, have high levels of cellular-protective substances.
